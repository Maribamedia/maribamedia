Frequently Asked Questions

What invoices qualify for discounting?

Government invoices must be issued to verified departments, municipalities, or public sector entities for delivered goods or services. Private invoices must be issued to credible, established corporate entities. In all cases, the work must be complete and the invoice verified.

Does the work need to be fully completed before funding?

Yes. The goods must be delivered or the services fully rendered before the applicable invoice can be discounted. We fund against verified completed work, not pending orders.

How much of the invoice value can be advanced?

We typically advance up to 70% of verified government invoices and up to 80% of verified private sector invoices. The balance is released to you once the debtor settles the invoice, less the agreed facility cost.

How is repayment structured?

The debtor pays the invoice in full. The proceeds are used to settle the funding provided, and the remaining balance is released to your business. In private sector deals, this can operate as a revolving facility across multiple invoices.

How long until funds are available?

Government invoice discounting is typically funded within 3–5 days of complete documentation submission. Private sector deals typically settle within 7–10 days, depending on debtor verification.
